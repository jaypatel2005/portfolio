03 · Projects

Selected work

01

Agentic AI Research Assistant

An autonomous multi-agent system that conducts an end-to-end scientific research workflow—from discovering emerging domains and generating research questions to gathering evidence, designing experiments, critically evaluating results, and producing a structured research paper.

Built with a cyclic LangGraph architecture, the system uses specialized agents and a confidence-driven self-correction loop to replan when evidence is weak or contradictory. If sufficient confidence cannot be established, the system refuses to generate a final paper.

02

Medicinal Leaf Classification

During my undergraduate studies, I worked on a project aimed at identifying medicinal leaves from images. An initial model trained on a standard dataset plateaued at 77% accuracy, while training on a secondary dataset of pre-segmented, background-free images achieved 97% accuracy.

This raised an important generalization concern: a model trained on clean, segmented images could struggle with real-world photographs containing complex backgrounds. To address this, I proposed an intermediate preprocessing stage that uses object detection and image segmentation to automatically isolate the leaf before classification.

This approach improved the model’s robustness on real-world images by combining segmentation-based preprocessing with the classification pipeline.

03

AI Question Generator

An AI-based system designed to generate targeted questions for interviews, self-assessment, and examinations. The system can work with user-provided knowledge sources, including PDF documents, to generate questions grounded in specific learning material rather than relying only on generic prompts.

The project explores generative AI for personalized assessment by adapting question generation to a particular subject, knowledge base, or evaluation context. It also includes a question-review component intended to assess and refine generated output.
